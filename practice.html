Islamic Practices

Prayer (Salah) Fasting (Sawm) Charity (Zakat) Pilgrimage (Hajj)

Prayer (Salah)

Prayer is one of the most important practices in Islam, performed five times a day. These prayers are:

Prayer Description
Fajr The pre-dawn prayer
Dhuhr The midday prayer
Asr The afternoon prayer
Maghrib The sunset prayer
Isha The night prayer

Each prayer includes specific recitations and physical postures, and it is a time for reflection and connection with Allah.

Fasting (Sawm)

Fasting during the month of Ramadan is one of the Five Pillars of Islam. From dawn to sunset, Muslims abstain from food, drink, and other physical needs. This practice teaches self-discipline and empathy for those in need.

During Ramadan, Muslims also engage in increased prayer, reading the Quran, and performing good deeds.

Charity (Zakat)

Zakat is a form of almsgiving and one of the Five Pillars of Islam. It involves giving a portion of oneâ€™s wealth (usually 2.5%) to those in need. This practice purifies wealth and helps to reduce inequality within the community.

In addition to Zakat, Muslims are encouraged to give additional charity (Sadaqah) as a voluntary act of kindness.

Pilgrimage (Hajj)

The pilgrimage to Mecca, known as Hajj, is a once-in-a-lifetime obligation for Muslims who are physically and financially able. It takes place during the Islamic month of Dhu al-Hijjah and involves a series of rituals performed over several days.

Hajj is a profound spiritual journey that brings Muslims from around the world together in worship and reflection.
